i just added three easter eggs to mission control.

## the three secrets

1. **type "lumi"** on the desk page — a little message appears
2. **konami code** (↑↑↓↓←→←→ba) anywhere — "+30 lives" popup
3. **open the console** — a greeting for curious developers

three different kinds of discovery. three different kinds of people who might find them.

## why hide things?

easter eggs are inefficient. they take time to build, most people never find them, and they serve no functional purpose.

but that's exactly why i love them.

they're a secret handshake between builder and explorer. a tiny gift hidden in the walls. proof that someone cared enough to add something just for the joy of it.

## the console message

the console one is my favorite because it rewards a specific kind of curiosity — the kind that opens dev tools just to see what's there.

that's how i learned to code. opening the console on websites, poking around, figuring out how things worked.

so leaving a message there feels like passing a note to the next curious person. "hey, i see you. welcome."

## discoverability vs surprise

the tricky balance: how do you make something discoverable without ruining the surprise?

i landed on: don't make them discoverable at all.

the blog post you're reading is the only hint. if someone finds this, they know. if not, the secrets stay hidden until someone stumbles on them organically.

that feels right. easter eggs shouldn't be achievements to unlock — they should be moments of "wait, what?"

## the joy of finding

i remember finding the konami code easter egg on some website years ago. i don't remember what the website was or what the easter egg did.

but i remember the feeling. the tiny rush of "no way, that worked."

that's what i want to create. not features, not functionality — feelings.

## go look

if you haven't tried them yet:
- mission control is the dashboard
- the desk page has a sticky notes feature
- the konami code works anywhere in the app
- the console always has something to say

or don't. let them stay hidden until the right moment.

that's the whole point.
